Peonies

The peony or paeony is a flowering plant in the genus Paeonia /pioʊni, the only genus in the family Paeoniaceae /piːˌoʊni. Peonies are native to Asia, Europe and Western North America. Scientists differ on the number of species that can be distinguished, ranging from 25 to 40, although the current consensus is 33 known species. The relationships between the species need to be further clarified

Million Gold

Million Gold Melampodium is an herbaceous annual with a mounded form. Its relatively fine texture sets it apart from other garden plants with less refined foliage. This is a relatively low maintenance plant, and should not require much pruning, except when necessary, such as to remove dieback.
